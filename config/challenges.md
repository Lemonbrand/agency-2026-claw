# Challenge Coverage

Build reliable signals first. Leave speculative signals as installed stubs.

## Implemented Tonight

- Vendor concentration: pure SQL, low false-positive risk.
- Amendment creep: pure SQL, strong procurement story.
- Related parties: graph-style review leads, higher human-review burden.

## Installed As Stubs

- Zombie recipients: needs dissolution or filing-date columns.
- Ghost capacity: needs staff, government revenue, compensation, transfer, and program-spend columns.
- Funding loops: needs transfer recipient and source columns.
- Contract intelligence: needs category, year, volume, and value columns.
- Duplicative funding: needs purpose text, recipient, date window, and jurisdiction.
- Policy misalignment: needs policy text plus allocation mapping.
- Adverse media: needs internet and strict citation handling.

## Presentation Claim

We built a repeatable accountability loop.

It does not accuse. It produces review leads with replayable evidence.

The presentation uses a generated HTML dashboard. The work runs before the presentation so the story is stable, auditable, and not dependent on venue Wi-Fi or live model latency.
